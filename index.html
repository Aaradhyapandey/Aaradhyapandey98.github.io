Hello, I'm Aaradhya, a third-year graduate student at Princeton University interested in probability theory. I am supervised by Professor Sanjeev Kulkarni.

My research focuses on exploring various aspects of probability theory and its applications in real-world scenarios. Currently, I am working on questions of clustering both for graphs and for Euclidean data. The relevant questions fall under the broad area of statistical inference on graphs, often goes by the name of community detection and has been extensively studied in the framework of the stochastic block model. The clustering questions for Euclidean data have been studied for Gaussian mixture models.

Recently, I got interested in understanding the mathematics of data visualization, and in particular the t-SNE method. It does produce amazing pictures and brings out the cluster structure in the original high-dimensional data. I hope to understand the mathematics of this dimension reduction technique in the future.
